A GPU, or graphics processing unit, is a specialized processor designed to perform the complex mathematical calculations needed to render images and video. GPUs are an essential component of modern computers and are particularly important for gaming and other graphics-intensive tasks. They are also used in various other fields, including scientific research, machine learning, and cryptocurrency mining.
GPUs are designed to be highly parallel, meaning that they can perform many calculations simultaneously. 

This allows them to quickly process large amounts of data, making them much faster than traditional CPUs (central processing units) for specific tasks. GPUs are often used in conjunction with CPUs in modern computers, with the CPU handling the overall control and coordination of functions and the GPU running the more specialized and data-intensive calculations.
